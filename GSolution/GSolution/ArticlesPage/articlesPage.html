Overfishing: Understanding the Problem and Its Impacts

Overfishing, catching fish faster than they can reproduce, is driven by demand for seafood, technology, and subsidies. It leads to declines in fish populations, reduced food security, and environmental degradation. WWF promotes sustainable practices, science-based catch limits, and consumer education to reduce overfishing.

The biggest threat to life below water: Apathy

The biggest threat to life below water is apathy. Many people have become desensitized to the problems our oceans face, which include pollution and overfishing. Collaboration and individual actions are needed to create sustainable solutions that protect and conserve our oceans..

"The Impact of Plastic: National Geographic Education on Ocean Pollution"

The National Geographic Education article, "Ocean Trash: 5.25 Trillion Pieces and Counting, Big Questions Remain", highlights the scale of ocean pollution caused by plastic waste. The article discusses the impact on marine life and ecosystems, as well as potential solutions such as bioplastics and policy changes. The article emphasizes the importance of continued research and awareness to address this global problem.
